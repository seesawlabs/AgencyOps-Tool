# AgencyOps Creative Brief

## Goals
- Close more business
- Maximize margins
- Increase customer satisfaction
- Create predictability within our business
- Give company ability to scale
- Create marketing efficiencies

## Overview
SeeSaw Labs needs a unified internal platform to manage the full lifecycle of agency projects, from first contact through final delivery, closeout, and case study creation. Today, critical workflows across pre-sales, sales, resourcing, account management, delivery, margin tracking, marketing follow-up, and project closeout are fragmented across documents, emails, spreadsheets, and manual processes. This creates inefficiencies, weakens visibility, increases delivery and profitability risk, and makes it harder to scale a consistent client experience.

## Problem
The current operating model does not provide a single source of truth for project and client lifecycle management. Teams spend too much time researching prospects, preparing for calls, evaluating fit, pricing work, coordinating documents, tracking hours, monitoring budgets, managing milestones, and closing out projects. Important information is often scattered, hard to retrieve, or inconsistently captured. As a result, SeeSaw Labs faces challenges in qualifying the right opportunities, staffing effectively, executing projects on time and on budget, protecting margins, maintaining strong client relationships, and systematically capturing outcomes for future marketing and growth.

## New Business
**Summary:** Prospect research and call prep take too long, lead qualification is subjective, and pricing/scoping lacks consistency.

- It takes too long to research prospects, their industries, and their likely pain points in order to prepare tailored call guides for qualification conversations.
- There is no consistent, objective way to score opportunities and determine whether a lead is a strong fit for SeeSaw Labs.
- Risk of pursuing poor-fit opportunities for too long before disqualifying them.
- Estimating project pricing is too manual and inconsistent, which makes it difficult to protect margins and confidently scope work.
- Important pre-sales information is often spread across notes, emails, and documents, making it hard to maintain a complete view of each opportunity.
- Limited visibility into conversion rates by lead source, service type, or industry.
- Difficulty connecting prospect needs to the most relevant case studies, capabilities, or service offerings.

## Resource Planning
**Summary:** There is limited visibility into team allocation, availability, utilization, and staffing needs across projects.

- There is limited visibility into who is working on which projects, in what capacity, and across what timelines.
- It is difficult to understand team availability, utilization, and bandwidth when planning new work.
- Resource planning is not tightly connected to project timelines, milestones, or expected effort.
- No clear way to forecast staffing needs for upcoming work.
- Difficulty identifying over-allocated or underutilized team members.

## Account Management
**Summary:** Client-facing documents, contract execution, renewals, and invoice tracking are managed too manually.

- There is no single, organized repository for client-facing documentation across the life of a project.
- Preparing, routing, and executing SOWs and MSAs between SeeSaw Labs and clients is too manual.
- It is difficult to track contract milestones, renewals, and other service-related deadlines.
- There is no central place to monitor outstanding invoices, overdue invoices, and account status.
- Client communications, approvals, and key decisions may not be captured in a single system.
- Difficult to see account health at a glance across active clients.
- Manual follow-up is required for missing approvals, unsigned documents, and unpaid invoices.
- Limited visibility into upcoming expansion, renewal, or risk signals within existing accounts.

## Project Delivery
**Summary:** It is difficult to see whether projects are aligned to scope, on schedule, and within budget.

- There is no clear, consistent way to monitor whether project delivery is aligned with the original SOW.
- It is difficult to know in real time whether a project is on schedule.
- It is difficult to know in real time whether a project is on budget.
- Project progress, milestones, and deliverables are not always visible in one place.
- Limited visibility into whether client feedback or approvals are delaying progress.
- No standardized method for tracking deliverable completion and acceptance.

## Margin Management
**Summary:** Project costs, budget performance, and margin health are not visible in real time.

- There is no complete, real-time understanding of the costs associated with each project.
- It is difficult to compare budgeted effort and cost against actual effort and cost over the course of delivery.
- Margin performance is hard to predict early and difficult to monitor consistently once work is underway.
- Limited visibility into how staffing choices affect profitability.
- Leadership may lack a reliable way to compare profitability across clients, service lines, or project types.

## Marketing & Relationship Management
**Summary:** Follow-up with leads, former clients, and referral sources is inconsistent and not automated.

- There is no consistent, automated process for staying in touch with prospects, past clients, referral sources, and other key audiences over time.
- Outreach and follow-up efforts depend too heavily on manual effort and individual habits.
- Limited visibility into which follow-up efforts lead to meetings, referrals, or new opportunities.
- No standardized cadence for re-engaging dormant leads or former clients.

## Project Closeout
**Summary:** Handoffs, retrospectives, reporting, testimonial collection, and case study generation are not standardized.

- Project closeout is not always handled through a clean, consistent handoff and offboarding process.
- There is no standardized retrospective process to capture successes, issues, and lessons learned.
- Information needed for a future case study is not consistently captured during or at the end of a project.
- Creating case studies is too manual and often delayed because inputs are incomplete or scattered.
- End-of-project reporting on performance and financial outcomes is not standardized.
- Collecting formal client reviews and testimonials is not built into the closeout process.
- No consistent measurement of client satisfaction at closeout.
- Limited ability to compare estimated versus actual project outcomes after completion.

## Objective
Create a centralized platform that gives SeeSaw Labs a clear, repeatable, and measurable way to manage agency projects from opportunity through closeout. The system should reduce manual work, improve visibility, support better decision-making, strengthen delivery quality, and make it easier to scale operations while protecting margins.

## Audience
Primary users include sales, leadership, project managers, account managers, and delivery team members. Secondary users may include contractors, clients, and marketing stakeholders who need access to specific project outputs, documents, or closeout materials.

## Desired Outcome
The platform should serve as the operational backbone for SeeSaw Labs by creating one connected workflow across all project phases. Success means faster qualification, better pricing confidence, cleaner project setup, stronger delivery oversight, clearer financial visibility, more consistent closeout, and better reuse of project insights for future selling and marketing.

## Success Criteria
- Centralize project, client, and documentation workflows in one place
- Improve visibility into project status, resource allocation, costs, and margins
- Standardize lead evaluation, delivery tracking, and closeout processes
- Reduce manual administrative work across teams
- Support more consistent client experiences from first contact to final handoff
- Make it easier to capture project outcomes for reporting, reviews, and case studies

## Considerations
- This tool doesn’t need to have everything built but needs to at least have integrations into other sources of truth.
